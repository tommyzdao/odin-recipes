Gordon Ramsey's Tomato Risotto

Description

Risotto is a creamy Italian dish that tastes a lot like the rice version of mac and cheese. If you're going to learn a unique dish like this then why not learn a recipe from one of the best chefs in the world?

The secret to making good rissoto is to add your hot stock very gradually, stirring to make sure it has all been absorbed before adding the next ladleful. This way you can control the consistency better and ensure the rice still has a slight bite to it - al dente, as the Italians call it - when you take it off the heat. This classic rissoto just needs a garnish of roclet or baby spinach. For a British twist, use spelt barley instead of rice - it has a lovely nutty flavour

Ingredients

  • 2-3 tbsp olive oil
  • 200g risotto rice
  • 500ml chicken or vegetable stock
  • 50 unsalted butter
  • 250g cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 100g mascarpone cheese
  • 25g Parmesane cheese, grated
  • Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper

Here's how we make Gordon Ramsey's gorgeous tomato risotto

  1. Heat the oil in a large frying pan, add the rice and stir well to coat the grains in the oil. Bring the stock to the boil and add 1 ladleful of it at a time to the rice, stirring well after each addition, until the liquid has been absorbed and the rice is cooked, but still al dente. (This will take 15-18 minutes)
  2. Meanwhile, heat the butter in a small saucepan, add the tomatoes and gently cook for about 10 minutes until soft. Pass through a mouli or coarse sieve; alternatively, mash with a potato masher.
  3. When the rice is cooked, fold in the mascarpone, Parmesan and tomato mixture, adjust the seasoning and serve
